A friend in need is a friend indeed

Companionship in Nature

Posted by Pragya Rawat on September 24, 2016

Lichens occur on rocks, leaves, and even on barks but more often than not they escape our notice and we miss out on one of the most brilliant relationships between two separate groups of organisms which occurs naturally. A lichen is a composite organism that emerges from algae or cyanobacteria living among the filaments (hyphae) of two fungi in a mutually beneficial symbiotic relationship.

A symbiotic relationship is one in which two or more organisms of different species are in an association of mutual benefit or dependence.

A lichen is a composite organism that emerges from algae

In the case of lichens, the fungi benefit from the carbohydrates produced by the algae or cyanobacteria via photosynthesis. The algae or cyanobacteria benefit by being protected from the environment by the filaments of the fungi, which also gather moisture and nutrients from the environment, and provide an anchor to it.

The world around us is actually full of such associations such as the partnership between the pistol shrimp and the gobies, sharks and the remora fish, the ostrich and the gazelle to name a few.

Symbiotic associations may take place between organisms for nutrition or protection and are based on the complementary characteristics of participating members such the ostrich’s good sight and the gazelle’s excellent hearing that makes them a stronger survival pair against the predators of the Savannah.
