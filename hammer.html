Hammerhead Planarians

Earthworm-eating, land-dwelling, terrestrial planarians.

Posted by Pragya Rawat on September 24, 2016

I saw a land planarian on my back from the Living Root Bridges in Nongriat. It had an anchor shaped head with a long and slender body. The body was orange in colour marked with black stripes and that ended at the base of its head part in the form of a collar.

I recognised this animal as an annelid because of a similar method of locomotion. It raised its head and waved it around a bit, in a surveying manner before bringing it down and dragging its body forward. I found this manner acutely similar to that of an earthworm and hence the assumption that it was also one.

It raised its head and waved it around a bit, in a surveying manner before bringing it down and dragging its body forward.

On further research I found out that it belonged to phylum Platyhelminthes of family Geoplanidae, genus Bipalium and species B. kewense.

Geoplanidae is a family of flatworms which are large in size and terrestrial hence they are commonly known as land planarians or land flatworms. While the Bipalium genus is identified exclusively by the shape of its head because of which they are also known as hammerhead worms.

I identified this animal as Bipalium kewense
